Short Bio

I am an incoming Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Louisiana Tech University. I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of South Florida (USF) in Fall 2023, under the supervision of Dr. Hao Zheng, with research contributions in ML for EDA: specification mining and high-level synthesis. I also hold an M.S. in Computer Engineering from USF and a B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering from Khulna University of Engineering and Technology.

Prior to joining Louisiana Tech, I was a postdoctoral research associate at the University of Central Florida, with a courtesy appointment at the University of Florida. I worked with Dr. Rickard Ewetz at the AI and Emerging Computing Lab on the DARPA-funded ANSR project, where we developed neuro-symbolic AI algorithms to enhance autonomous drone perception in resource-constrained environments using ROS2 and Docker-based systems.

I also interned at Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories (MERL) in Cambridge, MA, under the mentorship of Dr. Toshiaki Koike-Akino, where I worked on FPGA-based real-time signal processing accelerator design and optimization.

Research Interests

My research interests are centered around the development of secure, efficient, and evolvable computing and cyber architectures using machine learning. Specifically, I am interested in the broader areas:

  • Computer Aided Design (CAD) for VLSI
  • Computer Architecture
  • Computer Vision
  • Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning

Additionally, I specialize in applying AI to hardware-software co-design, multi-objective optimization in HLS, and micro-architecture design for energy-efficient SoCs. Through my research, I aim to push the boundaries of what is possible in computing, making computing more secure, efficient, and adaptable to future challenges.
