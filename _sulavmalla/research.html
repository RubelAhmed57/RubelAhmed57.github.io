Research

My research interest is broadly in the energy efficiency and power management of data centers. Data centers consume about 2% of the planet's electricity. In the U.S. alone, data centers consumed about 70 billion kWh electricity in 2014. My research focus is to explore ways to make data centers more energy efficient as well as to reduce their energy use. I have developed metrics to identify energy efficient servers for a data center.

I am also investigating ways to oversubscribe the data center power hierarchy. Data centers are expensive infrastructures and cost of building a data center can range from $10 to $25 per watt (that puts the cost of building a 1 MW data center between $10 Million to $25 Million). Since servers do not consume their rated peak power all the time, data center owners are incentivized to oversubscribe (install more servers than allowed by the power capacity limit) their existing power infrastructure to increase data center power infrastructure utilization and to lower the Total Cost of Ownership (TCO). Currently, I am investigating how the server energy proportionality can effect possible power oversubscription of a data center.

I have done work in safe oversubscription of power infrastructure in multi-tenant data centers (MTDC). An MTDC is a data center where the operator owns the infrastructure (building, power, and cooling) and leases the facility to multiple tenants. Tenants, who pay a monthly lease bill, may have their own servers installed and in turn provide a service to their own customer. One important distinction is that, the operator does not have direct control over a tenant's servers or workload. This requires new solution and mechanisms, compared to operator owned data center, such that the operator can have coordination and distributed control of tenants in an MTDC.
